My trip to Peru!

Hey there! Here is a little rundown of a trip that I took back in 2015 where my now wife(Stella) and I started dating. Peru is a magical place with a lot to do. There is everything from bustling cities to the most amazing natural features. The nature aspect of Peru is awe inspiring. From the Nasca lines and Machu Picchu to the Amazon rain forest. The cities are great as well. Some of the worlds best food and world renound chefs.

Lima Peru

We began our trip in Lima Peru. While in Lima we made our way to our hostile in Mira Flores. The Taxi ride there was deffinitley interesting. It was like being in the Fast and furious movies. When we arrived we got changed and went out to explore the night life. We wound up meeting up with a guy that Stella's brother met on whatsapp. We met at a nightclub which was full of foriegn exchange students from all over the world. We had a blast watching the live band. After we got an amazing burger in a pita style bread as we were wallking out of the club. The next day we got up early and walked around in a park that goes up and down the coast. There was art and food vendors out. We stopped and got an delicious empanada (pictured here). Then it was off to Cusco.

Cusco Peru

We arrived in Cusco after Lima. We checked into another hostile which only cost us about $30 per night. The accomidations were a little different than what one might be used to as an American. Luckily we did get our own room, but the bathroom was shared with all the guests. We then met up with Stella's brother and his friend who also came along on the trip. We went out that night to a resteruant recomended by some people in the town. Her brothers friend was not feeling well so we went without him. I got an alpaca steak and quinoa salad (pictured below). We only stayed here for the night and then we headed on the hike of a lifetime.

Day One Salkantay Trail

After a good meal and a decent nights sleep we went to meet up with her brother and his freind at their hostile. When we arrived we found them both still in thier beds. We found out that both of them were up all night with a stomach bug(I'll spear the graphic details). Needless to say they missed the hike. The trail guides came and picked us up from the hostile and drove us to the trailhead. So, at this point we had only been in Peru for two whole days and in the mountains for one day while in Cusco. We got to the trailhead and started or five day adventure. Pitured below is the start of this hike. We were feeling good at this point and just enjoying the scenary. That mountain in the back of this photo is where we were going. It is called Salkantay. The peak is 20,574 ft above sea level. We got about half way up it this day. We hiked for most of the day to get to camp. The altitude was already starting to get to me at this point. I decided to stay in the tent that evening. Stella decided to go on a side excursion to a little lake on the side of the camp but further up the mountain. I was not feeling well so I went to dinner and stayed in the tent during the mingle time. Later Stella came back to the tent and we tried to fall asleep. I bet we slept for about two maybe three hours and then I woke up to Stella outside the tent with altitude sickness(again sparring details). Needless to say this was day one.

Day Two Salkantay Trail

Day two was probably the hardest day of the hike. We were awakend with coca tea brought to the door of the tent by the trail guides. We were then provided breakfast and warm tea. It was pretty cold at this point. Stella was still not feeling well. They took the people with altitude sickness on donkeys to the top, and Stella got to ride one. Although wouldn't call it a win with how she was feeling. Here is a photo of me before we started this days hike. This is to this day the hardest hike I have done. The altitude got me like I've never felt before. We deffinitley should have spent more time acclimating. There was a point where I cound't take more than ten to fifteen steps without gasping for air. But, we finally made it to the top. We didn't make it to the summit but pretty close. Once we were at the top they let us enjoy the views for a bit, but then we booked it down to the next camp. We decended from a mountainous environment which gave way to the jungle and totally different vegitation.
